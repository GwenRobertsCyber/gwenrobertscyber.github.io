Deep Dive into Digital Forensics Exploring WinHex

If there is one area of cybersecurity that completely has my attention right now, it is digital forensics. There is just something incredibly satisfying about treating a compromised system like a digital crime scene. Recently, I have been going hard on learning WinHex in my home lab. Being able to look directly at the hex values of a file and understand what is actually going on under the hood is wild.

For a recent personal project, I practiced identifying altered file signatures. Malicious actors will often change file extensions to hide malware, but if you know how to read the hex headers, they cannot hide the true nature of the file. This kind of deep level analysis is exactly what I want to be doing in my career to help secure our national infrastructure. It is definitely a steep learning curve, but taking the time to understand the absolute basics of how data is stored and manipulated is making me a much better investigator.

Next up on my lab schedule is trying to recover some purposely deleted files from a corrupted drive image, so I will definitely post an update on how that goes!
